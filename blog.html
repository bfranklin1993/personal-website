Design systems that scale

Published on: March 1, 2025

Building design systems that can grow with your company is challenging but crucial. I've found that focusing on fundamental principles rather than specific implementations leads to more adaptable systems.

The key is to create a system that designers want to use, not one they feel forced to adopt.

By establishing clear guidelines while allowing for contextual flexibility, we can build systems that support rather than constrain creativity.

Three principles I follow when developing design systems:

  1. Start with user needs, not component libraries
  2. Create strong documentation and examples
  3. Allow for evolution rather than enforcing strict compliance

Honest product design

Published on: February 15, 2025

What does it mean to create "honest" digital products? For me, it's about designing with user interests truly at heart, avoiding dark patterns, and creating interfaces that communicate clearly.

Transparency isn't just an ethical choice—it builds long-term trust and loyalty.

When users understand exactly what they're getting and how a product works, they develop deeper confidence in both the product and the company behind it.

Honest design means:

  • Setting clear expectations about what a product can and cannot do
  • Making costs, limitations, and data usage transparent
  • Creating interfaces that guide rather than manipulate

Simplicity as a practice

Published on: January 20, 2025

The hardest part of design isn't adding features—it's knowing what to remove. I've been exploring how constraints and intentional limitation can lead to more powerful, focused products.

Simplicity isn't just an aesthetic; it's a practice that requires constant vigilance.

Every new feature or element we add comes with a cognitive cost for users. Great design is often about reducing options to only what's truly necessary.

I've found that regular design audits help maintain simplicity. Set aside time each quarter to review your product and ask: "What can we remove without compromising core functionality?"
